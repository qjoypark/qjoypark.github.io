Ph.D. Candidate · Lake Restoration Ecology

Yuying Guan

Doctoral researcher at the Nanjing Institute of Geography and Limnology, Chinese Academy of Sciences, investigating how hydrological fluctuations reshape riparian ecosystems and nutrient cycling in large lakes.

Research Focus
Ecological water-level regulation, lake riparian resilience, eutrophication mitigation, and dissolved organic matter dynamics in plateau and lowland systems.
Methodological Strengths
Phytoplankton taxonomy, long-term water-quality monitoring, R-based data analytics, ArcGIS remote sensing workflows, and in situ mesocosm experimentation.
Field Experience
Extensive riparian and watershed surveys across Chaohu, Gaoyou Lake, Erhai, and the Yangtze Basin, integrating sediment cores, vegetation transects, and optical sensing.

Research Outline

Linking hydrological variability with ecological restoration strategies to stabilise lake ecosystems.

Riparian Hydrology Experiments

Designs mesocosm experiments and quantitative models to identify ecological water levels that support reed communities and nutrient retention along Chaohu shorelines.

Optical Signatures of DOM

Combines satellite imagery, in situ spectroscopy, and fluorescence indices to decode dissolved organic matter sources across plateau lakes in Southwest China.

Research Projects

Cross-disciplinary collaborations spanning national water-pollution control programs and ecological restoration pilots.

“Three Waters” Eutrophication Control

Documented phytoplankton succession and nutrient gradients in Liangxi River, delivering datasets for river-lake ecological engineering under the 13th Five-Year Water Special Program.

Chaohu Riparian Restoration

Captured high-nutrient sediments using dredging slot techniques, quantified contaminant removal efficiency, and assessed algal seeds to inform lake shoreline rehabilitation.

Inland Water Remote Sensing

Calibrated Landsat and Gaofen imagery with field observations to retrieve chlorophyll-a and CDOM in Erhai, supporting eutrophication diagnostics.

Yangtze Basin Ecological Tracking

Surveyed seasonal wetland extent, vegetation assemblages, and ecological water levels for the Yangtze River ecological restoration tracking initiative (2022-2023).
